Canson

The covers

Canson is one of the oldest paper mills in Europe. Founded in 1557 by the Montgolfier family of Annonay (the same family that flew the first hot-air balloon in 1782). Notebook covers are cut from Montgolfier, a heavy artist sheet that takes ink and wash without buckling. Card covers and postcards use Montval, a thicker watercolour paper with a tighter grain that holds saturated washes better at small format. Both age with the work.

Japan · Sakae Technical Paper

Iroful

The paper inside

Iroful is a fountain-pen paper from Sakae Technical Paper, the mill behind Tomoe River. At 75 gsm it feels smooth but not slick. Fountain pen ink sits flat: no feathering, no bleed-through, clear sheen on the reverse. If you have a favourite pen or a habit of pressing too hard, this paper has you covered. Forty sheets inside every notebook and every Le Carnet.

Russia · St. Petersburg · since 1934

Nevskaya Palitra

The paint

The watercolours are White Nights, produced by Nevskaya Palitra in Saint Petersburg. The name (Белые Ночи) comes from the summer weeks when the northern sun never really sets over the Neva. The pigment load is unusually high, so colour stays saturated even when watered down for a wash. It keeps its depth rather than fading to a pale stain.

France · Fil Au Chinois · 150+ years

Sajou Lin Câblé

The binding

Every notebook and card is stitched closed with Lin Câblé, cabled waxed linen thread made in France by Fil Au Chinois under the Sajou name. Two already-twisted threads wound together, then passed through a bath of starch and beeswax. The result is slightly rigid, smooth, and extraordinarily strong: it holds the binding for decades without stretching or fraying.
