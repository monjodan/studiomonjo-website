Paper and pages

Certified paper, chosen for ink.

The cover gives each notebook its mood. The interior paper carries your writing. It has to feel substantial, take fountain-pen ink cleanly, and make sense as a responsible paper choice.

The Robey editions and the Monjo Line share the same writing-first paper system, so choosing between them is a question of story, not quality.

The writing surface

The quiet luxury is how ink behaves.

The page should not feel coated, slippery, or disposable. It should give the nib a little texture, keep the line crisp, and let ink settle with the small pleasure many of us remember from school notebooks, dip pens, and fountain pens.

That memory is useful, not nostalgic decoration. Robey gives it somewhere to become a journal, a travel note, or a gift. Monjo Line brings it into the workday: meetings, plans, lists, drafts, and the private thinking that still happens better on paper.

Paper standards

The paper certification.

The interior writing paper is FSC certified, ECF, and acid free. This certification promise refers to the paper used for writing inside the notebook.

FSC

Responsible forest sourcing

FSC certification gives paper a traceable connection to responsibly managed forest material through the supply chain.

ECF

Elemental chlorine free

ECF means the paper is whitened without elemental chlorine gas, reducing the impact of the bleaching process while keeping the sheet clean for writing.

pH

Acid-free paper

Acid-free paper is less likely to yellow or become brittle over time, so the notebook keeps better as an object you may want to keep.
