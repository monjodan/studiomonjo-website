Notebooks

Hand-painted notebooks, in editions of fifty.

A single watercolour painted by hand, scanned, and printed onto fifty more notebooks. Each one is then finished to your direction at order — your thread, your paper style. Two sizes. Made in Seoul.

The process

A cover, painted from start to seal.

Each cover begins as a blank sheet of heavy artist paper. Ink line first, then watercolour, then a small vermillion seal. There's no sketch underneath. The line lands or it doesn't.

When the original notebook is finished, the watercolour is scanned at high resolution and printed onto fifty more. Each one is stab-bound by hand, numbered inside, and finished with the thread and paper you choose at order.

How it writes

Made for the page being written on.

The paper inside is chosen for one thing: the way a fountain-pen line lands on it. Each edition uses a Japanese fountain-pen paper — kind to wet ink, kind to slow strokes. Watercolour holds. Pencil reads. The page doesn't bleed.

Two sizes — a small one for the pocket, a larger one for the desk. What changes between editions: the painting on the cover, sometimes the paper weight, sometimes the colour of the thread. What doesn't: it's made for writing in. See the materials ›

The edition

One original. Fifty prints.

Each edition begins as one watercolour painted by hand in the studio. That first notebook — the one with the original watercolour on the cover — is one of a kind and sold as the original artwork.

The watercolour is then scanned at high resolution and printed onto fifty more notebooks, numbered 1 to 50, with the date and the studio mark inside. When the fifty are gone, that's it. No reprints, no replacements. A new painting begins.

Finished to your direction

Each one a little bit yours.

Every notebook in the edition is finished to your direction at order. You pick the thread colour for the binding and the paper style for the inside.

What I choose, per edition, are the things that have to live with the painting: the cover paper for the way it carries the watercolour, and the inside paper colour — cream or white — for the way it sits with the cover. Two layers of choice, each in its proper place.

By hand

A person makes this. That used to go without saying.

Each cover is the trace of a decision: which way the brush goes, where the line stops, what a hand chose to keep. There's no template, no preview, no second take. The line lands or it doesn't.

What changes from one cover to the next is what changes when a person paints something. What doesn't change is the care. Some things, still, ask to be made slowly, by hand, by someone, for someone.

Materials

What it's made of.

Each notebook is built from four materials. Some are chosen by me for each edition — the cover paper and the inside paper colour, both picked to suit the watercolour. Some are chosen by you at order — the thread colour and the paper style. Below is the small palette they all come from.

Heavy artist paper · chosen per edition
The cover
Painted in watercolour, then printed

The cover paper is chosen for each edition to suit the watercolour — a thicker, more textured sheet for a richer palette, something smoother and brighter for a quieter painting. The original is painted on the sheet directly; the fifty that follow are high-resolution prints onto the same paper. Made to be painted on, not written on.

Fountain-pen paper · cream or white per edition · style at order
The paper inside
Made for the act of writing

Japanese fountain-pen paper, smooth but not slick. Ink sits flat: no feathering, no bleed-through, a clear sheen on the reverse. Watercolour holds. Pencil reads. I choose the colour for each edition — cream or white, depending on what sits with the cover. You choose the style at order, from the options available for that edition.

Artist watercolours · Russia
The paint
Pigment that holds its depth

Professional artist watercolours from a tradition that goes back to the early twentieth century, named after the summer weeks when the northern sun never really sets. The pigment load is unusually high, so colour stays saturated even when watered down for a wash. It keeps its depth rather than fading to a pale stain. The vermillion seal on every cover comes from this same set.

Waxed linen thread · France · colour at order
The binding
Stab-bound by hand, visible on the spine

Cabled waxed linen thread, made by a French house that has been turning thread for more than a century and a half. Two already-twisted strands are wound together, then passed through a bath of starch and beeswax. The result is slightly rigid, smooth, and extraordinarily strong — it holds the stab-binding for decades without stretching or fraying. You choose the colour at order. The default is vermillion red, which echoes the seal on every cover.
