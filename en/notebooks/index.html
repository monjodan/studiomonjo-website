Notebooks & cards

Hand-painted notebooks and cards, made one at a time in Seoul.

Notebooks (A6, A5), bound cards (A6), postcards, and seasonal boxed sets. Forty pages of fountain-pen paper inside every notebook. Each design is an edition of twenty.

The process

A card, painted from start to seal.

Each cover begins as a blank sheet of Canson. Ink line first, then watercolour, then a small vermillion seal. There's no sketch underneath. The line lands or it doesn't.

Once the cover is dry, the notebook is folded, sewn closed by hand with French waxed linen, numbered inside, signed.

Made of

Four makers, one studio.

Iroful paper inside, from the Sakae mill that also makes Tomoe River. Heavy Canson on the covers. Russian White Nights watercolours. French waxed linen on the spine. See the materials ›

Materials

Four makers, one studio.

Every Studio Monjo piece is assembled from a small set of carefully chosen materials, each with decades or centuries of craft behind it.

France · since 1557
Canson
The covers

One of the oldest paper mills in Europe, founded in 1557 by the Montgolfier family of Annonay (the same family that flew the first hot-air balloon in 1782). Notebook covers are cut from Montgolfier, a heavy artist sheet that takes ink and wash without buckling. Card covers and postcards use Montval, a thicker watercolour paper with a tighter grain that holds saturated washes better at small format.

Japan · Sakae Technical Paper
Iroful
The paper inside

A fountain-pen paper from Sakae Technical Paper, the mill behind Tomoe River. At 75 gsm it feels smooth but not slick. Fountain pen ink sits flat: no feathering, no bleed-through, clear sheen on the reverse. If you have a favourite pen or a habit of pressing too hard, this paper has you covered. Forty sheets inside every notebook.

Russia · St. Petersburg · since 1934
Nevskaya Palitra
The paint

The watercolours are White Nights, made in Saint Petersburg. The name (Белые Ночи) comes from the summer weeks when the northern sun never really sets over the Neva. The pigment load is unusually high, so colour stays saturated even when watered down for a wash. It keeps its depth rather than fading to a pale stain.

France · Fil Au Chinois · 150+ years
Sajou Lin Câblé
The binding

Every notebook and card is stitched closed with Lin Câblé, cabled waxed linen thread made in France by Fil Au Chinois under the Sajou name. Two already-twisted threads wound together, then passed through a bath of starch and beeswax. The result is slightly rigid, smooth, and extraordinarily strong — it holds the binding for decades without stretching or fraying.
