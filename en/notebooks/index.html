The first story

Robey and the Falling Petals.

Robey came from far away with quiet habits: writing slowly by hand, keeping old receipts inside books, and stopping to look at light on walls.

In Seoul, Robey began noticing small things other people seemed to walk past. A flower through cracked pavement. Tangerine peels near a window. Petals gathering silently around a bench.

The writing surface

The quiet luxury is how ink behaves.

The page should not feel coated, slippery, or disposable. It should let the nib move, keep the line crisp, and hold ordinary writing without easy bleed-through.

That is the standard behind the paper choice: not a decorative claim, but the part of the notebook the hand meets every day.

Paper standards

The paper certification.

The interior writing paper is FSC certified, ECF, and acid free. This certification promise refers to the paper used for writing inside the notebook.

FSC

Responsible forest sourcing

FSC certification gives paper a traceable connection to responsibly managed forest material through the supply chain.

ECF

Elemental chlorine free

ECF means the paper is whitened without elemental chlorine gas, reducing the impact of the bleaching process while keeping the sheet clean for writing.

pH

Acid-free paper

Acid-free paper is less likely to yellow or become brittle over time, so the notebook keeps better as an object you may want to keep.
