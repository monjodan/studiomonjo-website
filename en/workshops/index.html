Workshops · Seoul

Bind it. Paint it.
Take it home.

A three-hour class. You'll bind a notebook from sheets of paper, paint its cover in ink and watercolour, and leave with the one you made.

What you'll leave with

One notebook, made by your own hand.

Hand-bound, hand-painted, signed and dated. Same paper Studio Monjo uses for the editions, same thread, same ink. Yours from the first stitch to the last brushstroke.

Three hours from blank sheets to a finished cover. No experience required. Tea on the table.

What we'll do

Three hours, one notebook.

  1. STEP 01

    Cut and fold the paper

    We start with sheets of fountain-pen-friendly paper. You'll measure, cut, and fold them into the signatures that become your notebook.

  2. STEP 02

    Stitch the binding by hand

    I'll show you a simple Coptic-style stitch. By the end you'll have a bound notebook, sewn closed with French waxed linen thread.

  3. STEP 03

    Paint the cover

    Then we paint. Ink first, then watercolour. I'll guide you through composition, brushwork, and how to let the page do half the work.

  4. STEP 04

    Sign it. Take it home.

    You leave with a notebook you bound and painted yourself, signed and dated, ready to write in.

Good to know

A few details.

Do I need any experience?
No. The workshop is built for first-timers. If you've never held a brush or threaded a needle, you'll still leave with a finished notebook.
What language is the workshop in?
English or Korean, whichever the group prefers. I run both.
What's included in the price?
All materials (paper, thread, ink, watercolour, brushes), the studio space, tea, and the notebook you make. Nothing extra to bring.
Can I book a private session?
Yes. For couples, small groups, or one-on-one, write to me and we'll find a date.
