Workshops · Seoul

Eight seats at the Monjo table.

A small workshop in Seoul. Learn to write with a dip pen, stitch an A6 pocket notebook, and leave with its first page already written.

What you'll leave with

Leave with a notebook and a nib.

You fold, stitch, and sign an A6 pocket notebook, then write its first page with a dip pen. The writing paper and waxed thread come from the same family as Studio Monjo editions. The nib, the pen holder, and the practice guide go home with you.

No experience required. Left-handed writers are welcome. The class moves slowly, with guide sheets under every line.

The material lesson

Materials first.

You learn why each material is there: what paper does for ink, why thread needs wax, and how a dip pen line changes with the weight of your hand.

Paper

Fold sheets chosen for writing and feel how grain, thickness, and surface change the finished notebook.

Thread

Stitch with waxed linen so the binding stays firm, visible, and honest about how the notebook is held together.

Ink

Dip the pen and learn how thick and thin lines give letters their rhythm.

What we'll do

Two and a half hours, a pen and a notebook.

  1. STEP 01

    Learn the dip pen

    Start with the pen: how to hold it, how to dip, how to draw a line. Guide sheets keep every stroke on track.

  2. STEP 02

    Stitch the binding by hand

    Fold the paper, align it, and sew it with waxed thread. By the end you hold an A6 pocket notebook bound by your hand.

  3. STEP 03

    Write the first page

    In the notebook you just bound, with the pen you just learned, write a line you love.

  4. STEP 04

    Sign it, date it, take it home

    Sign it, date it, and take it home wrapped in translucent paper. The nib and holder go with you.

Good to know

A few details.

Do I need any experience?
No. First-timers are welcome. You will still leave with a finished notebook.
I write with my left hand. Will it work?
Yes. The nib angle and your seat at the table are adjusted for left-handed writing.
What language is the workshop in?
English or Korean, whichever the group prefers. I run both.
What's included in the price?
Paper, thread, the dip pen and ink, guide sheets, tea, and the notebook you make. The nib and holder you write with are yours to keep. Nothing extra to bring.
Can I book a private session?
Yes. For couples, small groups, or one-on-one, write to me and we'll find a date.
