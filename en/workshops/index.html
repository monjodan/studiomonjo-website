Carnet de voyage.

Bind the journal you will travel with, at a French atelier in Seoul. Cover, thread, pages, colophon, all by your hand. Then the trip fills it: notes, sketches, watercolour, tickets glued in.

What you'll leave with

Leave with the journal you will travel with.

You fold an A5 journal, sew it with a thread color you choose, and make the Canson cover a memory of your trip: a line in ink, a wash of colour, a date. The writing paper and waxed thread come from the same family as Studio Monjo editions.

The pages are made to hold a whole trip: write, sketch, lay a light wash of watercolour, glue in tickets and stamps. No experience required; a line template card is there if you want guidance, and the last page is signed like an edition: made by hand in Seoul.

The material lesson

Materials first.

You learn why each material is there: what paper does for ink, why thread needs wax, and how ink and watercolour sit together on a cover.

Paper

Fold 105 g sheets chosen for writing, thick enough for ink, a light wash of watercolour, and a ticket glued in.

Thread

Stitch with waxed linen so the binding stays firm, visible, and honest about how the notebook is held together.

Cover

Draw a line in ink, add a wash of colour, and make the cover yours.

The atelier

How a notebook is born here.

Every edition starts as a painting, becomes a printed Canson cover, and waits on this table as flat sheets: folded, gathered, sewn with its own color of thread, then signed and wrapped.

Your carnet takes the same path. Nothing is pre-made; you begin from flat paper, exactly where every notebook here begins.

What we'll do

Two and a half hours, your carnet.

  1. STEP 01

    The story and the paper

    Tea first. The cahier where Monjodan learned to write, why a French binder works in Seoul, and Robey’s letters on the table. Then the 105 g paper in your hands.

  2. STEP 02

    Your cover first

    A line in ink, a wash of watercolour, the date of your trip. The Canson sheet that wraps your journal becomes your first memory of Seoul.

  3. STEP 03

    Fold and sew

    Fold the paper, align it, and sew it around your cover with the thread you choose. Red, orange, navy, black.

  4. STEP 04

    Sign it, date it, take it home

    Sign the last page, add the date and the place. One copy, made by hand in Seoul, by you. It goes home wrapped in translucent paper.

Good to know

A few details.

Do I need any experience?
No. First-timers leave with a finished journal. A line template card is there if a blank page makes you nervous.
What language is the workshop in?
English or French. The maker is French; Korean works too.
What's included in the price?
Paper, thread, ink and watercolour, tea, and the journal you bind. Nothing extra to bring.
Can I paint and paste in it?
Yes. The 105 g paper takes ink and light watercolour washes well, and glued-in tickets, stamps and photos are part of the carnet tradition.
When in my trip should I come?
Early. The journal is made to be filled while you travel. Bind it on day two, and the rest of your trip writes itself into it.
Can I cancel?
Full refund until 24 hours before the session.
Can I book a private session?
Yes. For couples, small groups, or one-on-one, write to me and we'll find a date.
