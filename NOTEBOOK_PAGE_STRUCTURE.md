# Monjo (notebooks) — Page Structure

**Purpose:** Rebuild `/en/notebooks/` and `/ko/notebooks/` as the centre of gravity for Studio Monjo's first focused product line: the Monjo notebook. Replaces the current page. Reads top to bottom as a single editorial story, not a catalogue.

**Underlying brand strategy:** `BRAND_STRATEGY.md`. Read that first if you haven't.

---

## Reading the page in one breath

A visitor arriving here should, in order, see: **the object → why the object exists → how the object is made → what's currently for sale → the story of the maker → where to buy.** Then they leave. That's the whole page. No nav at the top other than the floating *← Studio Monjo* pill we already use. No sidebar. No upsell.

---

## Section 1 — Silent open

**One full-bleed photograph of a finished Monjo notebook standing upright on a wooden surface.** Use the new photo `DSCF1070.jpg` (the water-drop cover photographed against the studio shelving with the ink bottles softly out of focus behind it). No text overlay. No headline. Just the object.

Below the photograph, after generous white space, a single line of italic Cormorant Garamond:

> *Painted by hand. In Seoul.*

That's the section. Nothing else.

**Why:** The first thing on the page should be the work, not a sales argument. By the time the visitor scrolls past this, they already understand more than any paragraph could explain.

Two CTAs, side by side:

**Why:** This is the only section where the brand speaks directly. After this it shows rather than tells.

## Section 3 — How it's made (the process)

This section earns the price. Four photo-text pairs, alternating image-left / image-right. Each pair pairs **one of the new making photos** with two or three sentences of plain copy.

Layout: two-column grid on desktop (image + text), single column on mobile (image, then text below).

**Pair 1 — The painting.**
Image: `paintings/L1000120.jpg` (sketchbook open, watercolour palette visible, bird with red flower).
Copy:
> *Every cover begins with a brush, on a single sheet, on this table.*
> *There is no sketch underneath. The line lands, or it doesn't.*
> *Once the painting is done, it is photographed and scanned at high resolution for the print run.*

**Pair 2 — The paper.**
Image: One of the new product photos showing pages or paper texture clearly. (If none exists, use `notebooks/IMG_4605.jpg`.)
Copy:
> *Inside: Iroful paper, from Sakae Technical Paper — the mill that also makes Tomoe River.*
> *Chosen for one reason: it holds fountain-pen ink flat. No feathering. No bleed-through.*
> *If you write with a wet nib, it has you covered.*

**Pair 3 — The binding.**
Image: `DSCF1074.jpg` (the red thread close-up against cream pages — a stunning shot).
Copy:
> *Hand-stitched in Japanese stab-binding, with red waxed linen thread by Sajou.*
> *The red spine is the only one of its kind in your bag.*

**Pair 4 — The seal.**
Image: A close-up of the vermillion seal stamp on a finished notebook. (Use `paintings/L1000132.jpg` cropped, or shoot a new one specifically.)
Copy:
> *Every Monjo notebook is signed with the same vermillion seal carved for the studio.*
> *It is the maker's mark. It means I made this. It is on the painting, on the cover, on the back inside page.*

**Why:** Four moves, four photographs, four short proofs. By the end of this section the visitor knows the object is real, knows what it's made of, and knows who made it. This is the section that converts.

## Section 5 — One painting. Fifty notebooks. Then it's closed.

This is the most important new section on the page. It explains the model and elevates it to brand promise.

**Layout:** Two photographs side by side. Left: the original watercolour painting (un-cropped, with the studio mess around it — use `paintings/L1000132.jpg` or similar). Right: a stack or fan of finished notebooks (shoot if needed).

**Underneath, centred, italic serif large:**

> *One painting. Fifty notebooks. Then it's closed.*

**Body copy (single column, max 580px):**

> *Each Monjo design exists as one original painting and fifty notebooks bearing that painting on the cover.*
>
> *The original is a piece of paper that was touched by a brush. There is one of it. It is sold once, to one person.*
>
> *The fifty notebooks make the same image accessible — fifty homes, fifty desks, fifty pens. Each is hand-bound, hand-signed, sealed, and shipped from Séoul.*
>
> *When the fiftieth notebook is sold, the edition closes. The painting moves to the archive. The next design begins.*

**Small note at the bottom:**

> *Originals are released through `studiomonjo.com/originals` and shown by appointment. [Ask about Édition N° 001 →]*

**Why:** This is the section that converts a "nice notebook" into a small art-edition object. It also seeds the future originals business without forcing it now.

## Section 6 — The inhabitants (the character system)

A short editorial moment introducing the recurring characters. This is the section that turns customers into collectors.

**Layout:** Three small painted vignettes in a row — the water-drop, the bird, the dragon — each isolated against cream paper. Below them, a single line:

> *Every Monjo edition has a small inhabitant somewhere in the painting.*
> *Sometimes they recur. Sometimes they appear only once. Each one is painted by hand and signed with the seal.*

This section should be the smallest on the page. One line of copy. Three illustrations. White space.

**Why:** Naming the cast turns it from "cute detail" into "deliberate practice." Returning visitors will start watching for it. Future editions get a built-in story hook ("Édition N° 003 — the one with the moon").

---

A short maker section. Not an "about" page — a single moment of who.

**Layout:** Profile portrait on the left (`media/web/profile/jordan.jpg`), short copy on the right.

> **Jordan Monnet.**
> *Founder, painter, binder of Studio Monjo. French. Based in Séoul since 2020.*
>
> *Before Monjo, Jordan worked in artificial intelligence. The studio exists in part as the other side of that work: objects that one person made with their hands, for someone else to write in by hand.*
>
> *Studio Monjo also publishes Courrier de Séoul, a monthly handwritten letter.*
>
> `[ More about the studio → ]`

**Why:** This is the only place the AI/handmade tension gets stated explicitly. Quietly, in passing, in a maker bio. Not in a manifesto. Not on a homepage banner. *In passing* is more powerful than declared.

## Implementation notes for the coding agent

- **Identity:** Continue using the warm-cream sub-brand styles already established on `/courrier/` and the existing `/notebooks/` rebuild. `body.notebooks` class, `--paper-warm` `#FAF6ED`, italic Cormorant Garamond for headings.
- **Floating chrome:** Keep the existing `.page-back` + `.page-corner` setup (back-link pill top-left, social + language pill top-right).
- **Image asset paths:** All new images are under `/media/`. The site currently references `/media/web/notebooks/` and `/media/web/photography/`. New images in `/media/paintings/`, `/media/profile/`, and the new `/media/photography/DSCF*` shots need to be:
  1. Copied or symlinked into `/media/web/...` following the existing convention, OR
  2. Have `.webp` variants generated alongside the `.jpg` for the `<picture>` tags.
- **Schema:** Drop the legacy `ItemList` schema (which referenced old shop SKUs). Replace with a single `Product` schema for Édition N° 001 plus a `BreadcrumbList`.
- **Materials modal:** Keep the existing materials modal — it's still relevant. Link to it from the "How it's made" section's paper paragraph rather than from inline text.
- **Mobile:** The two-column "how it's made" pairs collapse to single column. The "1 + 50" double-image stacks vertically. Section 8 stays full-bleed on all sizes — it's the moment that needs to feel cinematic on a phone too.
- **Performance:** All hero images need WebP variants. The full-bleed shots in Sections 1 and 8 must each be under 220KB at the served resolution.
- **What to retire from the current page:** The features-strip (the four-column "Paper / Pages / Cover / Edition" row) and the editions grid component. Both are replaced by Sections 3 and 4.

## Out of scope for this rebuild

- A shop / cart / checkout. We sell on Naver.
- An originals listing. Originals get their own page later (`/originals/`); for now they're mentioned in Section 5 with an email-to-ask CTA.
- Workshops, Courrier, About. They have their own pages.
- A newsletter. Don't add one until Jordan decides what the newsletter would actually do.

---

## Acceptance criteria

A reviewer should be able to confirm each of the following in five minutes:

1. The page opens with a single photograph and a single italic line — no nav heading, no hero copy block.
2. The "1 + 50" model is given its own dedicated section, not buried as a footnote.
3. The water-drop / character system is named and shown.
4. There are no buy buttons except the NaverShop link at the end and in Section 4.
5. The maker bio mentions the AI background once, in passing, with no banner or callout.
6. The page reads as a single editorial story top to bottom on a 1440px display, with no horizontal scrolling.
7. On a 380px phone, all photographs render full-width and all double-column sections stack cleanly.
8. Lighthouse Performance ≥ 90 mobile.
