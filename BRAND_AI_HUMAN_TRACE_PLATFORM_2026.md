# Studio Monjo AI / Human Trace Brand Platform

Date: June 9, 2026  
Purpose: define how Studio Monjo can lean into the contrast between artificial intelligence and hand-made writing objects without becoming anti-technology, generic, or sentimental.

## 1. The Core Insight

Studio Monjo becomes more memorable when the brand is not only about handwriting, Seoul, or stationery.

The deeper idea is:

> AI makes words abundant. The hand makes them yours.

This is the strongest philosophical spine so far.

It explains:

- why a person working with AI would make notebooks by hand
- why fountain pens and dip pens matter
- why paper still matters
- why a monthly physical letter is meaningful
- why workshops matter
- why Robey can exist as a small machine-like character who notices Seoul
- why Studio Monjo is not nostalgic, but contemporary

The brand should not say "AI is bad."

It should say:

> In a world where words can appear instantly, some words still deserve a hand, a page, a thread, an envelope, and time.

That is much more powerful.

## 2. The Brand Territory

### Territory Name

> The Human Trace

This can be the internal platform and sometimes an external phrase.

Why it works:

- it is broad enough for notebooks, letters, workshops, Robey, and founder story
- it is specific enough to separate Studio Monjo from normal stationery
- it does not reject AI
- it makes handmade objects feel necessary now, not quaint
- it gives the brand a strong emotional and philosophical core

### Public Line Options

Option A:

> For the human trace.

Option B:

> Writing objects for the human trace.

Option C:

> For what should remain handwritten.

Option D:

> In a world of instant words, keep a human trace.

Option E:

> AI makes words abundant. The hand makes them yours.

Option F:

> Seoul, written by hand.

### Recommended Architecture

Use a two-line system:

> For the human trace.  
> Notebooks, letters, and workshops from a French writing house in Seoul.

Then use the AI contrast in the supporting story:

> In a world where words can appear instantly, Studio Monjo makes objects that ask for the hand: pages to write in, letters to send, and a table where people make by touch.

This keeps the homepage memorable without overloading it.

## 3. The Founder Story

Your personal story becomes the proof, not the whole brand.

The lineage is unusually strong:

1. Your grandmother used a dip pen in school.
2. You used a fountain pen.
3. You later worked with artificial intelligence.
4. Studio Monjo now makes writing objects by hand in Seoul.

That is not nostalgia. That is a living history of writing tools.

The point is not:

> old tools good, new tools bad

The point is:

> every generation writes with its tools, but the human trace still matters.

### Founder Story Draft

> My grandmother learned to write with a dip pen. I grew up using fountain pens. Later, I worked with artificial intelligence, building systems that could produce language without a hand touching paper.
>
> Studio Monjo began from the tension between those worlds.
>
> I am not against AI. I work with it. But the more easily words appear on a screen, the more I care about the words we still choose to write by hand.
>
> A notebook keeps pressure, rhythm, hesitation, ink, crossings-out, and time. A letter carries distance. A workshop returns writing to the table, the body, and the hand.
>
> Studio Monjo is where those traces stay visible.

### Short Founder Bio

> Jordan Monnet is a French maker in Seoul. After years working with artificial intelligence, he founded Studio Monjo to make writing objects for the human trace: hand-bound notebooks, monthly letters, and small workshops for people who still write by hand.

### Sharp Founder Line

> I work with machines that produce language. Studio Monjo is where I make room for the human hand.

This is the sentence to keep.

## 4. Robey's Role

Robey becomes much more interesting in this platform.

He is not just a cute character.

He is:

> the small machine-shaped observer who learns Seoul through human attention.

Robey can hold the AI/human bridge without making the brand literal or heavy.

He is a projection of you:

- arriving in Seoul
- looking carefully
- not fully belonging at first
- discovering Korea through small scenes
- carrying a machine/AI-adjacent identity
- becoming more human through attention, drawing, writing, and making

Robey should not be explained like a mascot biography.

He should be felt through recurring behavior:

- Robey notices petals.
- Robey follows a balloon.
- Robey enters a small library.
- Robey pauses at a window.
- Robey watches rain on paper.
- Robey sees a letter leave Seoul.

### Robey Platform Line

> Robey is the small observer of Seoul: a machine-shaped character learning what only attention can keep.

### Simpler Public Line

> Robey notices the small things Seoul almost lets disappear.

### Why Robey Matters

Robey lets Studio Monjo say:

- AI can be near the brand
- machines are not the enemy
- attention is the human act
- Seoul is discovered slowly
- the hand is how the scene becomes yours

This is rare. Most craft brands are either anti-modern or purely nostalgic. Robey lets you be modern, tender, and strange in a good way.

## 5. Product Architecture Through The Human Trace

### Robey In Seoul

Role:

> the illustrated trace

Meaning:

Robey turns Seoul scenes into hand-bound notebooks. The product begins with observation and ends with the buyer's writing.

Product line:

> Robey noticed it first. You write what remains.

AI/human angle:

Robey is machine-shaped, but he does not generate. He notices. The notebook asks the human to finish the scene by writing.

### Monjo Line

Role:

> the thinking trace

Meaning:

Monjo Line is the quiet notebook for thoughts before or after the screen: meetings, ideas, plans, reading notes, sketches, prompts, edits, and decisions.

Product line:

> Before the laptop, one page.

AI/human angle:

This is the notebook for the part of thinking that should happen before it becomes a prompt, document, task, or output.

### Courrier De Seoul

Role:

> the sent trace

Meaning:

Courrier turns Seoul into a physical letter that travels to someone. It is the opposite of content feed speed.

Product line:

> A letter leaves Seoul.

AI/human angle:

A generated message can arrive instantly. Courrier arrives slowly, with paper, distance, signature, and the evidence that someone made and sent it.

### Workshops

Role:

> the embodied trace

Meaning:

Workshops let people return writing to the hand, table, tools, and material. They do not just learn a craft. They experience why the material matters.

Product line:

> Six seats at the Monjo table.

AI/human angle:

In a world where making can feel abstract, the workshop gives people one object they physically made from first fold to final cover.

### Words To Avoid Or Use Carefully

Avoid:

- soulless AI
- AI-proof
- anti-AI
- real humans only
- escape technology
- fight the machine
- authentic in a digital world
- handmade revolution
- analog rebellion

These are too easy and will date badly.

Use instead:

- not against AI, but for the hand
- instant words versus chosen words
- language with a trace
- the part of writing that remains physical
- where the hand enters the sentence

### Caption Lines

Use lines like:

> A prompt leaves no pressure mark. A pen does.

> Not against AI. For the hand.

> Some words should arrive with a crease.

> The screen gives speed. The page gives resistance.

> Before the output, a page.

> The hand slows the sentence enough to make it yours.

> Robey does not generate Seoul. He notices it.

> The thread holds what the screen cannot.

> A letter is a message with distance still inside it.

> In a world of instant words, this one took a hand.

## 8. Naver Implementation

Naver should use the AI contrast, but lightly.

The first job on Naver is still purchase clarity.

Use this kind of line near the top:

> AI로 글이 쉽게 만들어지는 시대에도, 손으로 쓰는 시간은 여전히 다릅니다.

Then immediately explain the product:

> 서울에서 손으로 접고 실로 제본한 만년필 노트입니다. A5/A6, 무지/줄/모눈 중 선택할 수 있습니다.

Do not make the product page too philosophical before the buyer understands the notebook.

## 9. Visual Implementation

Do not make the AI angle visually technological.

No neon. No circuits. No robot-future UI. No glitch typography.

The contrast should be shown through what AI does not have:

- pressure marks
- ink pooling
- thread tension
- page curl
- crossings-out
- paper fiber
- stamp texture
- envelope crease
- the hand entering the frame
- a table before and after making

The human trace is visual.

### Signature Image Ideas

1. A handwritten page beside a laptop, but the laptop is closed.
2. Fountain pen nib touching paper, with visible ink variation.
3. Thread being pulled through a notebook spine.
4. Robey looking at a Seoul scene, not a screen.
5. Courrier envelope with postal marks and hand signature.
6. Workshop table with six places set before people arrive.
7. Your hands folding paper, with tools around the page.
8. Dip pen / fountain pen / keyboard as a writing lineage image.

The last image could become iconic if photographed well:

> dip pen, fountain pen, keyboard, handmade notebook.

It tells the whole story without over-explaining.

## 12. Risk Management

### Risk 1: Sounding anti-AI

Avoid any framing where AI is the villain.

Studio Monjo is stronger because you know AI from the inside. You are not reacting from fear. You are choosing what belongs to the hand.

Preferred line:

> Not against AI. For the hand.

### Risk 2: Becoming too abstract

Every philosophical line needs an object nearby.

If you say "human trace," show:

- handwriting
- thread
- letter
- page
- ink
- hands
- workshop table

Never let the concept float without material proof.

### Risk 3: Overusing founder story

Use the founder story as the doorway, then return to the objects.

The sequence should be:

1. founder tension
2. object proof
3. buyer use
4. emotional meaning

Not:

1. founder monologue
2. founder monologue
3. founder monologue

### Risk 4: Making Robey too literal

Do not say Robey is AI.

Say he is machine-shaped, curious, small, and observant. Let people feel the link.
