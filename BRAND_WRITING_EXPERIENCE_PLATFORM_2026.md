# Studio Monjo Writing Experience Platform

Date: June 9, 2026  
Purpose: refine the brand platform around the full experience of writing by hand: pen, ink, paper, handmade object, imperfection, intention, gift, self-connection, and connection with others.

## 1. The Real Center

The brand is not only about words.

It is about the experience of writing.

The words are almost the last part.

Before the words, there is:

- the object
- the hand
- the paper
- the pen
- the ink
- the resistance
- the sound
- the page opening
- the thread holding
- the small imperfection
- the decision to write

This is the missing center:

> Writing begins before the words.

Studio Monjo is not simply selling notebooks, letters, or workshops. It is making the conditions for a person to enter writing by hand.

That is why the notebook matters. The page matters. The thread matters. The ink matters. The fact that it was made by a person matters.

## 2. The Stronger Brand Idea

The warmest and most ownable phrase is:

> From one hand to another.

This is stronger than "human trace" and warmer than "writing objects."

It contains:

- the maker's hand
- the writer's hand
- the gift
- the letter
- the workshop table
- the handmade imperfection
- the emotional connection
- the experience before the words
- the idea that every object is repeated, but never identical

It also has natural French and Korean versions:

French:

> De main en main.

Korean:

> 손에서 손으로.

This should become a central brand phrase.

Not necessarily the only headline everywhere, but the phrase that unlocks the world.

## 4. Why This Is More Right

### It starts with the object

The notebook is not a container for words. It is the first part of the writing experience.

The object invites a certain kind of writing.

A cheap notebook asks for nothing. A beautiful-but-precious notebook can make people afraid to begin. A Studio Monjo notebook should sit in the middle: special enough to make the act meaningful, usable enough to invite the first line.

### It includes imperfection without making it sloppy

The handmade object is not valuable because it is perfect.

It is valuable because it was made with intention.

One notebook may have the same size, same thread, same edition, same paper, same design. But because a person folded it, stitched it, checked it, packed it, it is not a clone.

Use this line:

> Same edition. Never the same object.

Or:

> Repeated by design. Never reproduced exactly.

This is a powerful distinction in an AI age.

### It makes the object a gift

Even when someone buys it for themselves, the notebook has the feeling of a gift.

Why?

Because a person made it before another person writes in it.

There is already an exchange inside the object:

> made by my hand, finished by yours.

This applies everywhere:

- notebook: maker to writer
- Courrier: writer to recipient
- workshop: maker to participant, participant to self
- Robey: observer to writer
- Monjo Line: thought to page before screen

## 5. The Role Of AI

AI is still important, but it should not be the emotional center.

AI is the context that makes the experience of writing feel more precious.

Do not position Studio Monjo as:

> handmade against AI

Position it as:

> writing by hand, after AI has made text effortless

The difference matters.

Use:

> AI can generate text. It cannot press a pen into paper.

> AI can produce words. It cannot give you the hesitation before the line.

> AI can help us write faster. Studio Monjo is for the writing we still want to feel.

> Not against AI. For the experience of writing by hand.

Avoid:

> soulless AI

> analog rebellion

> escape technology

> human trace as the main headline

The brand should feel calm, not defensive.

## 6. Founder Story

The founder story should become a lineage of writing experiences, not only writing tools.

### Founder Story

> My grandmother learned to write with a dip pen. I grew up using fountain pens. Later, I worked with artificial intelligence, building systems that could produce language without a hand touching paper.
>
> Studio Monjo began from the space between those experiences.
>
> I am not against AI. I work with it. But the easier it becomes to generate text, the more I care about the experience of writing: the pen, the ink, the paper, the pause before a line, the object made by one hand and completed by another.
>
> Writing by hand is how I return to myself. It is also how we reach others with attention.
>
> Studio Monjo is my way of making objects and moments for that experience.

### Short Founder Bio

> Jordan Monnet is a French maker in Seoul. After years working with artificial intelligence, he founded Studio Monjo to make notebooks, letters, and workshops for the experience of writing by hand.

### Sharp Founder Line

> I work with machines that produce text. Studio Monjo is where I return writing to the hand.

## 7. Robey's Role

Robey now becomes clearer.

Robey is not a mascot and not AI.

Robey is the projection of the person discovering a place through attention.

He is:

- curious
- slightly outside
- observant
- gentle
- machine-like, but emotional
- a way of seeing Seoul slowly
- a way of entering the page before words

Robey's job is not to explain the notebook. His job is to open the emotional door.

### Robey Line

> Robey notices first. You write what remains.

### Robey Explanation

> Robey began as a way to draw the feeling of discovering Seoul: small, curious, sometimes out of place, always looking carefully.

### Robey Experience

Robey makes the notebook feel already inhabited, but not finished.

The cover gives a scene. The writer gives the words.

## 13. Visual Direction

The visual system should now be judged by one question:

> Does this image make me want to touch the act of writing?

### Show More

- nib touching paper
- ink pooling and drying
- paper texture
- page turning
- thread tension
- hands folding paper
- hands stitching
- writing mistakes and corrections
- notebook edge and thickness
- story card inserted by hand
- envelope addressed by hand
- workshop tools before use
- finished notebook in someone's hands

### Show Less

- generic still life
- mood without use
- dark atmosphere where the object disappears
- perfect product photography that hides handwork
- abstract AI contrast
- empty pages without invitation

### Signature Image

The most important campaign image should be:

> dip pen, fountain pen, keyboard, hand-bound notebook, and a hand entering the frame.

This shows the lineage without explaining it.

### Product Image Principle

Show the product as both:

1. made object
2. writing companion

Not only one or the other.

## 14. Color Direction

The color system does not need to become louder.

It needs to feel more tactile.

Keep:

- warm paper
- ink black
- seal red
- graphite
- edition thread colors
- Courrier paper colors

Do not add "AI blue" or tech gray as a major palette.

The contrast with AI should come from materiality, not interface color.

### Color Roles

| Color | Feeling |
| --- | --- |
| Ink Black | intention and clarity |
| Warm Paper | interiority and touch |
| Seal Red | signature, care, final mark |
| Thread Colors | the unique life of each edition |
| Courrier Blue | distance, air, monthly arrival |
| Monjo Black | thought, work, seriousness |

### Important Rule

Let the handmade variations carry the color.

The thread, watercolor, ink, paper, and envelope should bring more emotional variation than the UI.
