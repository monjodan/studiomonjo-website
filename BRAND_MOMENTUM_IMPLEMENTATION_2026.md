# Studio Monjo Momentum Implementation Strategy

Date: June 9, 2026  
Purpose: translate the AI / human trace platform into a concrete brand, website, copy, SEO, ICP, color, content, Naver, and outreach system.

## 1. Strategic Thesis

Studio Monjo should not position itself as a handmade stationery brand.

That category is too broad, too tasteful, and too easy to forget.

Studio Monjo should position itself as:

> A French writing house in Seoul, making writing objects for the human trace in a world of instant words.

This is the sharpest platform because it connects everything that is already true:

- you work with AI
- you understand AI from the inside
- you are not anti-technology
- your grandmother used a dip pen
- you used a fountain pen
- you make notebooks by hand
- Courrier is physical, slow, signed, and sent
- workshops return people to the table
- Robey is a small machine-shaped observer discovering Seoul
- Seoul is the place where the brand becomes specific

The brand should not say:

> AI is bad.

It should say:

> AI makes words abundant. The hand makes them yours.

That is the wedge.

## 2. Why This Can Create Momentum Now

### Signal 1: AI is now ordinary, fast, and everywhere

Stanford's 2026 AI Index says generative AI reached 53 percent adoption in three years, faster than the personal computer or the internet. It also reports that generative AI is used in at least one business function at 70 percent of organizations, and overall organizational AI adoption continued to rise in 2025.

Source: Stanford HAI, 2026 AI Index, Economy chapter  
https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report/economy

Implication:

AI is no longer a niche tech story. It is becoming background infrastructure. A brand that says "AI exists, but the hand still matters" is timely without being trendy.

### Signal 2: People are not simply excited about AI

Pew's 2025 global AI survey found that across 25 countries, a median of 34 percent of adults are more concerned than excited about AI in daily life, 42 percent are equally concerned and excited, and only 16 percent are more excited than concerned. In no surveyed country was the largest share more excited than concerned.

Source: Pew Research Center, October 15, 2025  
https://www.pewresearch.org/global/2025/10/15/concern-and-excitement-about-ai/

Implication:

The public mood is ambivalent. Studio Monjo should not sell fear. It should offer balance: a place where AI can exist, but the human hand remains visible.

### Signal 3: Creators are using AI, but trust and control matter

Adobe's 2025 Creators' Toolkit Report surveyed more than 16,000 creators across the U.S., U.K., France, Germany, South Korea, Japan, India, and Australia. It found that 86 percent of creators use creative generative AI, while 69 percent are concerned about their content being used to train AI without permission. Adobe also frames creator preference around human-in-the-loop control.

Source: Adobe News, October 28, 2025  
https://news.adobe.com/news/2025/10/adobe-max-2025-creators-survey

Implication:

The winning posture is not anti-AI. It is "AI as tool, human as author." That is exactly your founder story.

### Signal 4: Handmade texture is a visible antidote to AI sameness

Etsy's Spring/Summer 2026 Seller Trend Report explicitly connects AI sameness with demand for handmade signals. It highlights stitched textures, human-made details, process shots, close-up photography, and descriptions that explain how and why something is made. It also identifies analog diary/journal behavior as part of a screen-heavy cultural response.

Source: Etsy Seller Handbook, March 17, 2026  
https://www.etsy.com/seller-handbook/article/1473931456647

Implication:

Studio Monjo should make thread, ink, folds, paper fibers, pressure marks, signatures, dates, and envelopes visually dominant. The human trace must be seen before it is explained.

### Signal 5: Korea has active demand for premium writing tools and personal decoration

The Korea Times reported that 29CM saw stationery and office-supply transactions rise 75 percent year over year from February 1 to 12, 2025. Premium fountain pens, ballpoint pens, and pencils rose 2.4 times; journals and planners grew 64 percent; notebooks grew 43 percent; Point of View sales grew more than 7.6 times in the cited period.

Source: The Korea Times, February 21, 2025  
https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/lifestyle/trends/20250221/diary-decoration-enjoys-continued-popularity-with-millennials-joining-trend

Korea JoongAng Daily also reported in January 2026 that customizable pens had become a fast-moving decorating craze, with short videos driving people to Dongdaemun and consumers using decorated objects as personal expression.

https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2026-01-14/culture/lifeStyle/Koreas-latest-decorating-craze-Customizable-pens/2498914

Implication:

Korea is not just practical office stationery. It has a strong identity-object and writing-tool culture. Studio Monjo should sell both utility and self-expression, but in a quieter, more adult register than fast decoration trends.

## 8. Website Visual Design

### Visual Principle

Do not visualize AI.

Visualize what AI does not leave:

- pressure
- ink pooling
- thread tension
- folded corners
- corrections
- paper fibers
- stamp texture
- envelope creases
- hands at work
- date and place marks

No neon. No code rain. No circuits. No glitch. No robot-future design language.

Robey can carry the machine metaphor gently. The brand design itself should stay tactile.

### Homepage Hero Image Direction

The best new hero image would be:

> a closed laptop, a fountain pen, a hand-bound notebook, visible thread, and one handwritten line.

Even better as a campaign image:

> dip pen, fountain pen, keyboard, hand-bound notebook.

That image tells the whole story:

- grandmother
- founder
- AI
- hand
- writing object

Use warm natural light, not theatrical darkness. The current candle/dark profile image is atmospheric, but too dark for the master brand direction. It can be used for editorial content, not as the central brand image.

## 9. Brand Color System

### Current Palette Diagnosis

The current palette is close. It has paper whites, warm creams, black ink, gray, and seal red. Keep it.

The danger is anonymity. Cream paper and elegant serif type can disappear into the premium-stationery fog.

The fix is not more colors. The fix is stricter roles and more visible signatures.

### House Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Primary ground | Paper White | `#FFFFFF` | product clarity, Naver, Monjo Line, image backgrounds |
| Warm ground | Warm Paper | `#FBF8F1` | homepage, house sections |
| Notebook ground | Hanji Cream | `#FAF6ED` | notebooks, Courrier, story pages |
| Deeper paper | Fold Shadow | `#EBE2CD` | panels, image placeholders, section contrast |
| Primary text | Ink Black | `#0A0A0A` | headings, logo, main text |
| Warm text | Warm Ink | `#1A1816` | notebook pages and poetic copy |
| Secondary text | Graphite | `#4A4640` | body copy |
| Quiet text | Pencil Gray | `#7A746A` | labels and metadata |
| Rule | Paper Rule | `#E6DFD0` | borders |
| Signature | Seal Red | `#B8231D` | seal, dot, stamp, one CTA accent |

### New AI / Human Trace Accent

Add one very restrained cool neutral:

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| AI contrast | Machine Gray | `#D8DEE2` | tiny background for AI/human comparison, never dominant |
| AI text | Screen Graphite | `#31383D` | labels in "before the screen" sections |

This gives the AI contrast a visual place without making the site look technological.

### Product Accent Colors

| Product | Accent | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Robey 001 Falling Petals | Petal Thread | `#B8231D` | thread marks, edition tag |
| Robey 002 Orange Balloon | Balloon Orange | `#D46B2A` | edition mark, thread close-up |
| Robey 003 Small Library | Library Navy | `#243A56` | edition mark, thread close-up |
| Monjo Line | Black Thread | `#111111` | identity line, buttons, thread motif |
| Courrier | Courrier Blue | `#496A8D` | monthly paper accent, postal marks |
| Workshop | Table Wood | `#8A6A4E` | sparing accent from photography, not UI dominant |

### Color Ratios

House pages:

- 70 percent paper/white
- 20 percent ink/gray
- 7 percent photography/material texture
- 3 percent seal red or product accent

Robey pages:

- 60 percent warm paper
- 20 percent product image
- 10 percent ink/gray
- 7 percent edition accent
- 3 percent seal red

Monjo Line:

- 75 percent white
- 20 percent black/gray
- 5 percent seal red or paper warmth

Courrier:

- 65 percent cream/paper
- 20 percent photography
- 10 percent blue/monthly paper
- 5 percent black/seal

### Color Rules

Use red like a seal, not a button color.

Use edition colors only where the edition exists.

Use cool gray only to make the AI/screen contrast readable.

Do not create rainbow stationery branding. The product can have color. The house should have restraint.
